📞 On The Call

Run through these before scheduling the estimate. Check each one off as you go.

🏠 Before You Show Up
📋 At The Walkthrough
💪 Close Mindset
You are not here to give a price. You are here to close a job.
If they say it's too much — ask what works for them, not why it's too much.
Silence after the price is okay. Let them sit with it.
Every objection is a question in disguise. Answer the question.
🥊 Objection Handler
💸 "That's too expensive"
tap
Don't defend the price. Ask a question.
"Compared to what? Is it the total number or the monthly impact that concerns you?"
"What were you expecting to invest in this?"
"I can look at trimming the scope — what's the one thing that matters most to you?"
Then use Close Tools in the Quote tab — offer a small discount to close today only.
🤔 "I need to think about it"
tap
"Think about it" means something specific is unresolved. Find it.
"Totally fair — what's the main thing holding you back right now?"
"Is it the price, the timing, or something else?"
"If the price and timing worked perfectly, would you be ready to move forward today?"
If they say yes to the last one — price or timing is the real objection. Handle that instead.
📋 "I'm getting other quotes"
tap
Don't panic. Separate yourself from the competition.
"Smart move — you should compare. Just make sure you're comparing the same scope. A lot of guys leave things out to get the low number."
"When are you looking to make a decision? I can hold your start date if you let me know by [date]."
"What would make you feel confident choosing us today?"
Create urgency around your schedule — you can't hold the date forever.
👫 "I need to talk to my spouse"
tap
This is why you confirm the decision maker is home before showing up.
"Completely understand — when can we get them on a quick call together? I can walk them through it in 5 minutes."
"What do you think their main concern will be — price or timing?"
"Are you personally happy with everything you've seen today?"
Get them to be your advocate with their spouse. If they love it, they'll sell it for you.
⚔️ "I got a cheaper quote"
tap
Never trash the competition. Let the logic do the work.
"How much cheaper? If it's a small difference I might be able to work with you. If it's significantly lower, they're either cutting corners on prep, using cheaper paint, or planning to rush the job."
"Did they spec out the same paint product and number of coats?"
"The cheapest paint job is the one you have to redo in 2 years."
If they're within $200-300 use your close discount. If they're way lower don't chase it — that's not your customer.
🤝 In-Person Close Playbook
Hesitates on price
Offer -$100 today only. "If we start within 2 weeks I can do $X."
Needs to think
"I can hold [date] for 48 hrs." Send GHL estimate before you leave.
Getting quotes
Hold firm. "Let me know what you get — I'm confident in our number."
Spouse not home
Send GHL from the driveway. Follow up in 24hrs not 72hrs.
Cheaper quote
Under $300 gap — match it. Over $500 — walk away, not your customer.
